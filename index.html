About Me

As a therapist, I care deeply about providing a safe, non-judgmental space for reflection, honesty, and vulnerability. I desire to see each individual discover their own self-worth by learning to cultivate courage & authenticity in all aspects of life.

Whatever you’re going through, you don't have to go through this alone. Taking this time right now to build on strengths and develop support will put you on your path of true wellness.

My style of therapy is to help people overcome obstacles and self-imposed barriers they may face so that they can flourish in their personal lives. My influences are vast and varied and include: Thich Nhat Hahn, Rene Descartes, William R Miller, Wayne McCashen, Viktor Fankl, Brene Brown, Gabor Mate, and William Glasser, to name a few.

I utilize an integrated approach that includes aspects of narrative therapy, emotion-focused therapy, CBT, DBT, Schema Therapy, and Motivational Interviewing. I specialize in treating addiction and dependence,and anxiety and depression. Although I am well educated in 12 step recovery, I am aware that the 12 step process does not work for everyone. I also have experience treating individuals with eating disorders and body dysmorphia, personality disorders, and those struggling with personal sexual identity/ orientation issues and interpersonal relationship concerns.

I have a Masters Degree in Social Work from The Ohio State University where I focused on mental health and addictions. I am an understanding, caring and passionate Licensed Independent Social Worker. I am told I have a way of getting people to open up and finally be honest with themselves.

Together we can work through whatever issue or issues are holding you back from being the best version of yourself. I look forward to working with you.

Experience & Education

I studied at The Ohio State University, Masters of Social Work (2016); Bachelors of Social Work (2014) where I focused on Behavioral Health, Substance Abuse: Treatment & Theory- Trauma-Informed Care, all aspects of Mental Health including personality and mood disorders.

I obtained an Associate of Arts degree at Columbus State where my studies included their Mental Health and Addiction Studies Program (2010).

I have worked for 13 years in addictions and mental health since I began my journey. I have done inpatient and outpatient treatment where I have witnessed people grow and overcome their personal struggles. Seeing people become the best version of themselves and developing into the person they were meant to be is one of life's great rewards that I treasure.
